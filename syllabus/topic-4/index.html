Topic 4: Statistics and Probability

4.1 Concepts, reliability and sampling techniques

  • Population, sample and random sample.
  • Discrete and continuous data.
  • Reliability and sampling methods.

4.2 Histograms, cumulative frequency graphs and box plots

  • Frequency distributions.
  • Histograms.
  • Cumulative frequency graphs.
  • Median, quartiles, percentiles, range and interquartile range.
  • Box-and-whisker diagrams.

4.3 Mean, median, mode, grouped mean, standard deviation, quartiles and IQR

Grouped mean

\bar{x}=\frac{\sum f_ix_i}{\sum f_i}

Interquartile range

\text{IQR}=Q_3-Q_1
  • Measures of central tendency and dispersion.
  • Standard deviation and variance using technology.

4.4 Pearson's, scatter diagrams and equation of y on x

  • Linear correlation of bivariate data.
  • Pearson's product-moment correlation coefficient.
  • Scatter diagrams and lines of best fit.
  • Regression line of y on x.
  • Interpreting correlation and limits of extrapolation.

4.5 Probability concepts and expected numbers

P(A)=\frac{n(A)}{n(U)}
P(A')=1-P(A)
  • Trial, outcome, sample space and event.
  • Expected number of occurrences.

4.6 Combined, mutually exclusive, conditional, independence and probability diagrams

  • Venn diagrams, tree diagrams, sample-space diagrams and tables of outcomes.
  • Combined events:
P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)
  • Conditional probability:
P(A\mid B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}
  • Independent events:
P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B)

4.7 Discrete random variables

E(X)=\sum x_iP(X=x_i)
  • Probability distributions.
  • Expected value and fair games.

4.8 Binomial distribution

X\sim B(n,p)
E(X)=np
\text{Var}(X)=np(1-p)

4.9 Normal distribution and calculations

  • Normal distribution and curve.
  • Normal probability calculations.
  • Inverse normal calculations where mean and standard deviation are given.

4.10 Regression line of x on y

  • Equation of the regression line of x on y.
  • Prediction using the appropriate regression direction.
  • Awareness that regression predictions cannot always be reversed.

4.11 Formal conditional probability and independence

  • Formal use of conditional probability formulae.
P(A\mid B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}
P(A\cap B)=P(B)P(A\mid B)
  • Testing for independence.

4.12 Standardization of normal variables

Standardized variable

z=\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}
  • Use of z-values.
  • Inverse normal calculations where mean or standard deviation is unknown.
