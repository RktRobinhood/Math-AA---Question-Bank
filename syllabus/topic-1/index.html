Topic 1: Numbers and Algebra

1.1 Scientific notation

  • Operations with numbers in the form a \times 10^k, where 1 \le a < 10 and k is an integer.
  • Calculator notation is not accepted as final scientific notation.

1.2 Arithmetic sequences and series

nth term

u_n = u_1 + (n-1)d

Sum of first n terms

S_n = \frac{n}{2}(2u_1 + (n-1)d)

or

S_n = \frac{n}{2}(u_1 + u_n)

Sigma notation

\sum_{i=1}^{n} u_i

1.3 Geometric sequences and series

nth term

u_n = u_1 r^{n-1}

Sum of first n terms

S_n = \frac{u_1(1-r^n)}{1-r}, \quad r \ne 1

1.4 Financial applications of geometric sequences and series

Compound interest

FV = PV\left(1+\frac{r}{100k}\right)^{kn}

Where:

  • PV = present value
  • FV = future value
  • r = annual interest rate (%)
  • k = compounding periods per year
  • n = number of years
  • Includes annual depreciation and other geometric financial models.

1.5 Laws of exponents with integer exponents and introduction to logarithms

Integer exponent laws

a^m a^n = a^{m+n}
\frac{a^m}{a^n}=a^{m-n}
(a^m)^n=a^{mn}
(ab)^n=a^n b^n

Definition

a^x = b \iff x = \log_a b \quad (a>0,\ a\ne1)

Natural logarithm

\ln x = \log_e x
  • Includes base 10 and base \(e\) logarithms.
  • Includes numerical evaluation of logarithms using technology.

  • Numerical and algebraic proof.
  • LHS-to-RHS proof structure.

1.7 Laws of exponents and logarithms

Rational exponent laws

a^{\frac{1}{m}}=\sqrt[m]{a}
a^{\frac{m}{n}}=\sqrt[n]{a^m}

Logarithm laws

\log_a(xy)=\log_a x+\log_a y
\log_a\left(\frac{x}{y}\right)=\log_a x-\log_a y
\log_a(x^m)=m\log_a x

Change of base

\log_a x=\frac{\log_b x}{\log_b a}
  • Includes solving exponential equations using logarithms.

1.8 Sum of infinite geometric sequences

Sum to infinity

S_\infty = \frac{u_1}{1-r}, \quad |r|<1

1.9 Binomial theorem

For n \in \mathbb{N}:

(a+b)^n=\sum_{r=0}^{n}\binom{n}{r}a^{n-r}b^r

Binomial coefficient

\binom{n}{r}=\frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}
