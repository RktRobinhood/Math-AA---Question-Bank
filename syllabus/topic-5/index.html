Topic 5: Calculus

5.1 Introduction to calculus

  • Limits from a table or graph.
  • Derivative interpreted as a gradient function and as a rate of change.
  • Notation including \frac{dy}{dx}, f'(x) and related contextual notation.

5.2 Increasing and decreasing functions

  • Graphical interpretation of f'(x)>0, f'(x)=0 and f'(x)<0.
  • Intervals of increase and decrease.

5.3 Differentiating polynomials, n in Z

\frac{d}{dx}(ax^n)=anx^{n-1}, \quad n\in\mathbb{Z}
  • Differentiating sums and multiples of polynomial terms with integer exponents.

5.4 Tangents and normals

  • Tangents and normals at a given point.
  • Equations of tangents and normals.

5.5 Integration introduction, areas between curve and x-axis

  • Integration as anti-differentiation for polynomial functions with integer exponents.
  • Anti-differentiation with boundary conditions.
  • Definite integrals using technology.
  • Area under a curve where f(x)>0.

5.6 Differentiating polynomials n in Q, chain, product and quotient rules

\frac{d}{dx}(x^n)=nx^{n-1}, \quad n\in\mathbb{Q}
\frac{d}{dx}(\sin x)=\cos x
\frac{d}{dx}(\cos x)=-\sin x
\frac{d}{dx}(e^x)=e^x
\frac{d}{dx}(\ln x)=\frac{1}{x}

Chain rule

\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx}

Product rule

\frac{d}{dx}(uv)=u\frac{dv}{dx}+v\frac{du}{dx}

Quotient rule

\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{u}{v}\right)=\frac{v\frac{du}{dx}-u\frac{dv}{dx}}{v^2}

5.7 The second derivative

  • Second derivative notation including \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} and f''(x).
  • Graphical behaviour and relationships between f, f' and f''.

5.8 Optimization, local extrema and points of inflexion

  • Local maximum and minimum points.
  • Testing for maxima and minima using the first or second derivative.
  • Optimization problems.
  • Points of inflexion and concavity.

5.9 Kinematics problems

v=\frac{ds}{dt}
a=\frac{dv}{dt}=\frac{d^2s}{dt^2}
\text{displacement}=\int_{t_1}^{t_2}v(t)\,dt
\text{distance}=\int_{t_1}^{t_2}|v(t)|\,dt

5.10 Indefinite integration, reverse chain and substitution

\int x^n\,dx=\frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+C \quad (n\ne -1)
\int \frac{1}{x}\,dx=\ln x+C
\int e^x\,dx=e^x+C
  • Includes composites with linear functions.
  • Integration by inspection or reverse chain rule.

5.11 Definite integrals, areas under curve onto x-axis and areas between curves

  • Definite integrals, including analytical approaches.
\int_a^b g'(x)\,dx=g(b)-g(a)
  • Areas under curves, including when f(x) is positive or negative.
  • Areas between curves.
