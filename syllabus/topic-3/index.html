Topic 3: Geometry and Trigonometry

Prior Learning (Assumed)

Area

A_{\text{triangle}}=\frac{1}{2}bh
A_{\text{parallelogram}}=bh
A_{\text{trapezoid}}=\frac{1}{2}(a+b)h
A_{\text{circle}}=\pi r^2

Volume

V_{\text{cuboid}}=lwh
V_{\text{cylinder}}=\pi r^2h
V_{\text{prism}}=Ah

3.1 3D space, volume, angles, distance and midpoints

  • Distance and midpoint in three-dimensional space.
  • Volume and surface area of 3D solids, including combinations of solids.
  • Angles between intersecting lines or between a line and a plane.

3.2 2D and 3D trigonometry, sine rule, cosine rule and area

Sine rule

\frac{a}{\sin A}=\frac{b}{\sin B}=\frac{c}{\sin C}

Cosine rule

c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos C

Area of a triangle

A=\frac{1}{2}ab\sin C

3.3 Applications of trigonometry

  • Applications of right- and non-right-angled trigonometry.
  • Pythagoras' theorem in context.
  • Angles of elevation and depression.
  • Bearings.

3.4 Circle: radians, arcs and sectors

Arc length

l=r\theta

Sector area

A=\frac{1}{2}r^2\theta

3.5 Unit circle definitions, exact trig ratios and ambiguous case of sine rule

  • Definitions of \sin\theta, \cos\theta and \tan\theta using the unit circle.
  • Exact trigonometric ratios.
  • Ambiguous case of the sine rule.

3.6 Pythagorean identity and double angles

\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta=1
\sin2\theta=2\sin\theta\cos\theta
\cos2\theta=\cos^2\theta-\sin^2\theta

3.7 Circular functions: graphs, composites and transformations

  • Graphs of \sin x, \cos x and \tan x.
  • Amplitude and periodicity.
  • Composite circular functions such as
f(x)=a\sin(b(x+c))+d
  • Real-life periodic models.

3.8 Solving trigonometric equations

  • Solving trigonometric equations in a finite interval, graphically and analytically.
  • Equations leading to quadratic equations in \sin x, \cos x or \tan x.
